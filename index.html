Overview

This research program investigates the emergence of spacetime geometry from quantum information-theoretic structures. We demonstrate how classical spacetime arises from quantum entanglement patterns and information processing constraints in quantum field theory.

Core Principle: Information-Energy Equivalence

$$E = Ic^2$$

Where $I$ represents information content, establishing the fundamental equivalence between information and energy.

Key Result: Spacetime geometry emerges from quantum entanglement patterns, providing a framework for quantum gravity based on information-theoretic principles.

Theoretical Foundations

1. Spacetime from Entanglement

Building on the ER=EPR correspondence, we show that geometric connectivity emerges from quantum entanglement:

$$ds^2 = \frac{\ell_P^2}{4} \sum_{i,j} S_{ij} dx^i dx^j$$

The metric tensor emerges from the entanglement entropy $S_{ij}$ between regions.

2. Holographic Emergence

The holographic principle suggests bulk physics is encoded on boundaries:

$$|\Psi_{\text{bulk}}\rangle = \sum_i \alpha_i |\phi_i\rangle_{\text{boundary}}$$

Bulk quantum states are reconstructed from boundary degrees of freedom.

3. Quantum Error Correction

Spacetime geometry provides natural error correction, explaining its stability:

$$|\psi_{\text{logical}}\rangle = \mathcal{E}(|\psi_{\text{physical}}\rangle)$$

The encoding map $\mathcal{E}$ protects quantum information, giving rise to stable geometric structures.
